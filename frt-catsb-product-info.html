The Microscope WLI is a white light interferometer mounted on an optical microscope. The 4-position turret allows operation with different objective magnifications.

The Microscope WLI is a white light interferometer mounted on an optical microscope. The 4-position turret allows operation with different objective magnifications. One objective position has a piezoelectric z - adjustment to hold an interferometric objective. Together with the CCD camera and the tool software, 3D information can be obtained from the surface. Depending on the objective used, interferometric measurements are performed with extremely high vertical and lateral resolutions. This allows for the precise measurement of roughness, step height, profile and 3D structures.

WLI features:>

  • high quality white light interferometer mounted on an optical microscope
  • 4-fold objective turret
  • easy to use, very high reliability
  • high performance hardware and software
  • non-destructive measuring
  • also available without an optical microscope (standalone version)
  • WLI upgrade of the existing microscope is possible

Outstanding solutions for specific needs

In addition to standard measuring systems, FRT designs and builds highly specialized solutions for applications in demanding production environments. Over many years, FRT has developed the competence to engineer and integrate metrological systems into third-party tools and to design entire process monitoring systems by carefully selecting only the best hardware and writing custom software. This is what we call "The Art of Metrology".

The features of custom solutions are:

  • specific customer design of a measuring system
  • customized automation solution
  • consulting and sensor selection

An example is the automated MicroProf® TTV. Initially the system was developed for the semiconductor industry for the high-precision measurement of wafer thickness and TTV, the system was adapted to cylinder head gaskets for inspecting roughness, step height and width. While a tactile system would take several hours for this measuring task, the especially adapted MicroProf® TTV could measure this application under 10 minutes. This enabled the customer to continuously monitor yield and the production machines. The MicroProf® TTV was developed as an easy-to-use solution. Automated measurement tasks were enabled with the software package with evaluation according to established industry standards.

The MicroSpy® Topo DT is a 3D microscope with confocal and interferometric measuring technology for fast and non-destructive surface investigations. Depending on the measurement

The MicroSpy® Topo DT is a 3D microscope with confocal and interferometric measuring technology for fast and non-destructive surface investigations. Depending on the measurement task the MicroSpy® Topo DT is operated as a confocal microscope or white light interferometer. Both methods are fast, without contact and non-destructive.

MicroSpy® Topo DT features:

  • Vertical-Scanning-Interferometry (VSI-Mode) for flat surfaces with sporadic, large vertical height differences
  • Phase-Shift-Interferometry (PSI-Mode) for very flat surfaces on glass, lenses, or polished wafers, especially for planarity measurements
  • confocal measuring mode: excellent depth of field without defocused image parts
  • 3D measurements of micro- and nanometer resolution
  • measurement of up to 37 mm ² in less than 10 seconds
  • 6-fold turret
  • high-grade computer with TFT monitor
  • motorized x,y precision sample stage

In development, production or quality assurance the measurement of flat, extended structures is problematic. Besides the roughness of the sample surface the form is also one of the most important parameters. Because the surface should be exactly up to the sub-μm range, smallest deviations from the ideal shape have to be determined. The MicroProf® 300 exactly provides these requirements and can be integrated into fully automated production.

MicroProf® 300 features:
  • high perfomance hard- and software
  • non-destructive measuring of roughness, waviness, contour, film thickness, topography and so on
  • upgradeable with additional point sensors and field of view sensors
  • large measuring area of 415 mm x 305 mm
  • powerful and long lasting LED light source
  • high measurement speed, max. 150 mm/sec

TTV Option

At any time, with the TTV option, the device can be upgraded for two-sensor inspection. Thereby a second CWL sensor is integrated and measures the components to be measured at the same point as the upper sensor of the underside. These, by the SEMI standards normalized development, are used for the measurement of thickness and thickness variation of wafers. Also other parameters like roughness can be measured at the same time on the underside.
